Solves Problems Involving Algebraic Expressions

INTRODUCTION

Solving problems involving algebraic expressions bridges the gap between basic arithmetic and advanced logical reasoning, providing a vital tool for applying mathematical logic to real-life situations. Since many real-world challenges are presented as verbal scenarios, developing the ability to analyze and translate these phrases into mathematical symbols is essential for navigating complex problems. To master this, we will utilize a structured approach to solve seven specific types of word problems found in instructional materials.

📐 Problem:

If the length of a rectangular vegetable garden is twice a number increased by 3 and its width is a number decreased by 5. Find the perimeter of the rectangle.

1
2
3
4
5
1

STEP 1 — Given & Asked

Given:

  • Length of the garden = twice a number increased by 3
  • Width of the garden = a number decreased by 5

Asked:

  • Find the perimeter of the rectangle
2

STEP 2 — Represent the Unknown

  • Let the unknown number be x.
  • Length (L) = 2x + 3
  • Width (W) = x − 5
3

STEP 3 — Solve

  • The perimeter of a rectangle is found using the formula 2(L + W).
  • Substitute the given expressions into the formula:
  • Perimeter = 2[(2x + 3) + (x − 5)]
  • Combine like terms inside the brackets: 2(3x − 2)
  • Distribute the 2 across the terms: 6x − 4
  • Expression: 6x − 4
4

STEP 4 — Answer

  • The algebraic expression for the perimeter of the rectangle is 6x − 4.
5

STEP 5 — Check

  • Adding all four sides manually: (2x+3) + (2x+3) + (x−5) + (x−5)
  • Combining the x terms (2x+2x+x+x) gives 6x.
  • Combining the constants (3+3−5−5) gives −4.
  • The resulting expression 6x − 4 matches the calculated perimeter.
  • The expression correctly reflects the given problem. ✅

"The length of a rectangle is x + 4 and the width is x. Write the expression of the perimeter."

  • Length of the rectangle = x + 4
  • Width of the rectangle = x

Asked:

Write the expression of the perimeter.

2

STEP 2 — Represent the Unknown

x + 4 = length of the rectangle

x = width of the rectangle

2(Length) + 2(Width) = perimeter of the rectangle

3

STEP 3 — Solve / Write the Expression

The perimeter of a rectangle is the sum of all its sides, or 2(L + W). Substitute the expressions:

Perimeter = 2[(x + 4) + x]

Combine like terms inside: 2(2x + 4)

Distribute the 2: 4x + 8

Expression: 4x + 8

4

STEP 4 — Answer

The algebraic expression for the perimeter of the rectangle is:

4x + 8

5

STEP 5 — Check

  • Adding all four sides: (x+4) + (x+4) + x + x
  • Combining x terms (x+x+x+x) gives 4x.
  • Combining constants (4+4) gives 8.
  • The expression 4x + 8 matches. ✅

2

x = total hours needed for the whole job

3

STEP 3 — Solve / Write the Expression

If a task takes x hours to finish, the work done in a single hour is the reciprocal of the total time.

Work per hour = Total Task / Total Time

Work per hour = 1 / x

Expression: 1/x

4

STEP 4 — Answer

The algebraic expression for the work done in 1 hour is:

1/x

5

STEP 5 — Check

  • If you multiply work per hour (1/x) by total hours (x), you get 1 (the whole task).

  • The expression 1/x correctly reflects the given problem. ✅
